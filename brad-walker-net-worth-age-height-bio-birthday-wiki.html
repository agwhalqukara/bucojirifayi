Brad Walker Biography

Brad Walker is one of the most popular and richest Association Football Player who was born on April 25, 1996 in Billingham, Billingham, United Kingdom. Bradley Paul Walker (born 25 April 1996) is an English professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for League One club Shrewsbury Town.

His form saw him nominated for the League Two Apprentice of the Year Award at the 2014 Football League Awards with Jack Whatmough and Ben Purrington. Walker won the award one day after scoring in a 4–0 win for Hartlepool against Bristol Rovers.

On 4 July 2017, Walker signed a two-year deal to join Crewe Alexandra. He scored his first Crewe goal, against Rotherham United in an FA Cup first round tie at Gresty Road, on 4 November 2017, and his first league goal for Crewe, against Grimsby Town, at Gresty Road on 1 January 2018.

On 30 August 2018, Walker joined Wrexham on a six-month loan. He scored his first goal for Wrexham, a penalty, in a 1–1 draw at Chesterfield on 27 October 2018.

Walker was born in Billingham, County Durham. He began his career with Hartlepool United and signed a professional contract with the club in July 2013. He made his Football League debut on 3 August 2013 in a 3–0 defeat away at Rochdale.
